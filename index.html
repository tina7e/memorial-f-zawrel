This page is dedicated to the memory of Friedrich Zawrel, whose life story serves witness to the cruelty of medicine and psychiatry under the ideology of "racial hygiene". It further illustrates that this ideology permeates the medical field far beyond 1945 allowing careers begun under its premise to prosper into our current millennium. Above all, it shows the necessity and importance of continued resistance.


Childhood

Friedrich Zawrel's childhood in Vienna is overshadowed by bitter poverty and the alcohol addiction of his father. Early on he is taken into foster care and spends periods in educational institutions. During a short-lived return to the family setting in 1940, Friedrich receives a letter that excludes him from joining any NSDAP organizations because of his father's exemption to serve.


When he doesn't join the Hitler Youth, Friedrich is ostracized and bullied by his classmates. He recounts that the other boys wagered that the reason for his exclusion couldn't be that he was Jewish because in that case "he'd be in a concentration camp". In his later years, Zawrel would point out how improbable it was that the general population wasn't aware of the Holocaust, when even school children knew about it.
